**I. About MODeL and the Courses**

What are UPOU MOOCs?
UPOU Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) are free, non-degree online courses designed to provide flexible, high-quality education to a global audience. Key features:

Massive: No enrollment caps—open to unlimited participants.

Open: No academic prerequisites or fees 

Online: Hosted on UPOU’s MODeL (Massive Open Distance eLearning) platform 

Course-structured: Designed with clear modules, learning outcomes, and assessments.



Why does UPOU offer MOOCs?

UPOU offers MOOCs to expand access to quality education, support lifelong learning, and enhance workforce readiness.



Who can enroll in UPOU MOOCs?

UPOU MOOCs are free and open to anyone with internet access, a willingness to learn online, and at least a high school education. There are no age, degree, or nationality requirements, and all the courses offer free certificates subject to completion of course requirements. 



These MOOCs cater to various learners, including students exploring subjects beyond formal curricula, professionals looking to upskill in specific areas of their work, marginalized groups such as rural communities, persons with disabilities, and career shifters, as well as international learners from the Philippines and beyond.



What are the technical requirements for a MOOC?

Internet Access – A stable connection is recommended, but low-bandwidth options are available.
Devices – You can access courses using a laptop, tablet, or smartphone.


What is the learning format of UPOU MOOCs?

UPOU MOOCs are fully online and self-paced, allowing learners to study anytime, anywhere. Courses include structured modules with video lectures, readings, and interactive activities (e.g., online quizzes with automated feedback)



Do MOOCs have a set schedule?

UPOU MOOCs are asynchronous, meaning there are no fixed class schedules requiring simultaneous participation. Learners can progress at their own pace within the course duration.



Do UPOU MOOCs have Instructors? How do they interact with learners?

Yes, UPOU MOOCs have instructors, also known as Course Coordinators, but direct interaction is limited due to the large number of learners.

Role of Instructors (Course Coordinators):
Update and maintain course materials.
Respond to questions that are not covered in FAQs or discussion forums.
Verify and certify course completers.
Modes of Communication:
Announcements – Used for important updates and reminders.
Discussion Forums – Encourages peer-to-peer interaction, with instructors moderating as needed.
   

Can MOOCs count toward a UPOU degree?

Currently, UPOU MOOCs do not earn credit toward formal degree programs in the university unless specified under program-specific policies.



What is MODeL?
MODeL is the official platform of the University of the Philippines Open University (UPOU) for Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs). Our courses are free and open to everyone. The courses we offer vary in topics such as Open Distance e-Learning, Teaching and Learning, Child Rights Protection and Promotion, Sustainable Development, Business Analytics, and many more.



How do I access and navigate UPOU MOOCs?

Step-by-Step Guide:

Visit MODeL – Go to UPOU's MOOC platform at https://model.upou.edu.ph/ 
Browse Courses – Explore available courses by category, such as Open and Distance eLearning, Business Analytics, or Sustainable Development.
Choose a Course – Select an available course that interests you.
Register – Create an account using your name and email.
Enroll – Click on your chosen course to gain immediate access.
Learn – Study through self-paced modules, including video lectures, readings, and quizzes. Participate in discussion forums if available.


What are your available courses?
You may check this link to see our updated calendar for this year:  https://model.upou.edu.ph/mod/page/view.php?id=2292. You may also go to https://model.upou.edu.ph, and scroll down to featured courses.



How long does it take to finish a course?
All of our courses are self-paced, so you may complete them at your own convenience. Each MOOC may require an average of three hours of study time per week. Most MOOCs run for 4–6 weeks and are self-paced. A typical workload is about 3 hours per week, meaning a 1-unit MOOC requires around 18 total hours. While deadlines are suggested, they are flexible, but all requirements must be completed by the course’s end.



How long is the duration of each course?
Courses run for three to eight weeks.



How much time should I dedicate weekly to a MOOC?

Time commitment varies per course, but as a general guide:

1-unit MOOC – Requires about 3 hours per week for 6 weeks (total: 18 hours).



Time Management Tips:

Set weekly learning goals (e.g., "Complete Module 2 by Friday").

Use downloadable checklists provided in course guides to track progress.



Is MODeL the same as MyPortal? As the Online Application System?
No, MODeL is UPOU's platform for MOOCs. MyPortal is a platform for students of UPOU degree programs. The Online Application System is a platform for those who are applying for admission to UPOU degree programs.



Can I put these MOOCs in my resume/CV?
Yes. You may put your participation in this MOOC in your resume or CV as a 16-hour training unless otherwise stated in the Certificate of Completion.



Why did a course vanish in my “My Courses”?
When courses end, we usually archive them so they become hidden. This allows the course coordinator to evaluate the submissions received in the course without any distractions and changes in the course site. This also limits students from submitting outputs beyond the deadline.



Are you accredited by CHED?
Yes. Our MOOCs are endorsed by CHED. You may also read about Republic Act 10650 to see how UP Open University is at the forefront of distance education in the country.



Are the MOOCs credited as CPD Units?
Unfortunately, CPD Units are not yet in place for our MOOCs, but you may include them in your resume or CV as a 16-hour training unless otherwise stated in the Certificate of Completion.



What materials and activities should I expect in the courses?
We use a variety of resources in various media such as videos, podcasts, and electronic print materials. Activities are also varied per course but you can expect some quizzes and assignments.



Do MOOCs have assignments or exams?

Yes, UPOU MOOCs include assessments, but they are designed for flexibility.



Formative Assessments (For Learning):

Auto-graded quizzes to check understanding.
Optional reflection journals for self-paced learning.
Summative Assessments (For Certification):

Online examination
Final projects such as case studies or video submissions.
Peer-reviewed assignments in select courses. 


How do I take an online exam in a MOOC?

You take the online exam on the site in the MODeL platform. Instructions on the exam schedule and format are posted at the start. Online exams are typically timed and auto-saved if unfinished.



Can I retake an exam if I fail or miss it in a MOOC?

Retakes are not allowed. Learners who fail or miss an exam can retake the course during its next offering.



What if I face technical issues during an exam in a MOOC?

Contact support immediately: Email model@upou.edu.ph.

Provide evidence: Screenshots of errors.

Alternative arrangements: Rescheduling may be offered for verified issues.



I encountered an error or bug on the platform. How can I report it?

Send a detailed email about the issue to model@upou.edu.ph, including screenshots if possible, your username, and the course name.



What support is available for learners in MOOC?
Technical Support: MODeL helpdesk for platform issues

Academic Support:

- FAQs and discussion forums.

- Automated feedback on quizzes.

- UPeng Chatbot that answers frequently asked questions

Accessibility:

- Screen-reader-friendly materials.

- Subtitled videos.

Note: Instructor interaction is limited due to the massive scale.



Can I do extra work to improve my class standing in a MOOC?

No, extra work is not allowed. All requirements for course completion and certificate awarding are clearly specified in the course. Learners must meet these requirements as outlined—additional tasks will not be considered. 



Can I download a course's materials and activities?
As long as the course material is downloadable (has a download button), you can download your course materials. Some course materials may be downloadable, while others can only be viewed within the platform. It depends on the course settings and the material type.



Can I access the course even after I finish it?

Yes. Once enrolled, you typically retain access to the course materials unless the course is archived or closed by the administrator.



I enrolled late and need more time to finish my course. Can you extend it?
We rarely provide extensions for our courses since everything is already scheduled per year. In cases where we have to extend a course, it will be decided by the course coordinator first.



Can you notify me if there are available courses?
You can visit the website from time to time or wait for announcements on our Facebook Page: UPOU Massive Open Distance e-Learning



Are the new Associate programs included in the 2023 MOOCs?
No. The new Associate programs are two-year pre-baccalaureate degree programs that ladderize to Baccalaureate degrees. All UPOU Associate programs will be delivered via Open and Distance eLearning, thereby offering flexibility for students. For admission details, please visit https://www.upou.edu.ph/academics/ or https://registrar.upou.edu.ph. You may also send an email to inquiries@upou.edu.ph.

Please send an email to model@upou.edu.ph.

**III. Enrollment**


How do I enroll in the courses?
To enroll in the courses, you can go to the homepage and scroll down to Featured Courses or MOOC Categories. From here, you can choose the courses you are interested in. Click on the title of the course so that you can get in.



How do I know if the course is open for enrollment?
Check the "Featured Course Offerings" on the MODeL homepage to see the date of availability of your chosen course.



How do I know if I'm already enrolled?
To know which courses you are enrolled in, go to "My Courses." The button is on the upper right of the screen.



I tried to enroll in the course, but it says “Unavailable." Why is this happening?
You may only enroll when the course opens for enrollment.



How can I unenroll myself in a course?
To unenroll yourself in a course, go to the course. Find Course Dashboard on the right side of the screen, below the list of topics, and click on it. At the center of the screen, you will find the “Unenroll Me” button that you have to click again. It will lead to a confirmation message. Just click “Continue” and you’re done.



Can I enroll even though the course has already ended?
If the course permits you to enroll, you can do that. However, the contents of courses that have already ended are most likely inaccessible.



I can't enroll in a course because it is asking for an enrollment key. I already have the badge so why is this happening?
Courses with enrollment keys are not open to the public or require registration to be manually enrolled in the course.



Can I enroll again next time if I don’t finish a course now?
Yes. You can enroll again. There are no limitations to the number of times you enroll in a course.



Can I enroll in more than 1 course simultaneously?
Yes. You can enroll again in more than 1 course.

**IV. Completion and Certificates**
Do I receive a certificate after completing the course?
Yes! E-certificates are awarded upon successfully completing course requirements, such as passing quizzes and submitting assignments. Requirements may vary for each course. We issue a certificate to completers at least two weeks after the end of the course. For more information, kindly read this: https://model.upou.edu.ph/mod/forum/discuss.php?d=19854



How do I know that I have already completed the course?
The easiest way to see if you have already completed is to check if your progress bar is already at 100%. However, there are times when the teacher has to acknowledge your submissions first so that they can be marked as complete. The best way is still for you to check manually if you did not forget anything.



How do I know that I am qualified to get the certificate?
More often, the easiest way to know that you are qualified to get the certificate is that you should have been able to submit the requirements needed to pass the course. You should also receive a passing grade on the assignments and quizzes. It is actually easier to determine the reasons that you may NOT be qualified:

You did not complete the requirements.
You did not carefully follow the directions.
You did not provide the right answer to the questions in the activities or your explanation is not valuable enough.
You committed plagiarism.
You copied your answers from somewhere or someone else.


When can I expect my certificate?
The certificate will usually be issued at least two weeks after the course duration. Time may vary depending on the number of students.



I completed the course but did not receive my certificate. What should I do?

Check if all course requirements are completed. If they are, wait for the system to generate your certificate. If the certificate still does not appear, contact model@upou.edu.ph with your username and course name.



What happens if I can’t complete a course?
If you don’t complete a course, you will not be able to get a certificate of completion.



The system is not acknowledging my submissions. Why does my progress bar say I am not yet 100% complete?
There are many possible reasons why your progress bar is not moving:

You did not follow the instructions to submit. For example, the direction says to post a discussion for your first requirement and reply to your discussion to submit your second requirement. If you did not follow the directions accurately, the system will not mark your submission as complete.
You did not pass the quiz. If you did not pass a quiz, it should have a red circle mark opposite its title.
You forgot to mark yourself as complete. Some courses have activities that require you to manually mark yourself as complete. These are activities that have a gray circle with a checkmark on the upper left part. When you click this, it will become a green circle with a checkmark.
You haven’t received a grade for a particular activity. Some activities require the teacher to manually acknowledge your submission.


The quiz marked me wrong even when I wrote the right answer. Why is this happening?
The prerequisite of being a distance learner is to adjust yourself to automated assessment. You have to be able to think about how a machine (usually run by artificial intelligence), rather than how a human, will read your answers. You have to be able to satisfy the machine logic rather than human emotion or logic. You have to think, “how do I satisfy the machine so that it counts my answers right?” One way is to provide the accurate words and phrases mentioned in the course.



While we recognize the limitations of automatic assessments run by artificial intelligence, for open online courses such as this one, this is just the first in the process and in no way dictates the decision of whether a participant satisfactorily meets the course requirements or not (thus, shall be given a completion badge/ certificate). The faculty course coordinators, at the end of the MOOC offering, still conduct the monitoring and double-checking of a participant's course activities before a completion certificate is released.



In terms of learning, regardless of the result of the automatic assessments, it is our hope that the open nature of the educational resources prepared and used for the MOOCs may also prove helpful for the participants as their study independently and or with the rest of the lifelong learners in the MOOCs.



Will I not get a certificate if I don’t have a passing grade on a quiz?
It depends on the requirement of the course. Contact your teacher. You can scroll to the bottom of a course page, and from there you can send a message to your teacher.

Why is my course progress labeled “Conditional”?
When a course is labeled as “Conditional,” it means that certain conditions have been set for it to be accessible. Usually, we use this to set the opening and closing dates of a course. When this is set, you can only access the contents for a certain period of time. 



What do I do after completing a course?
You can choose to enroll in another course or just wait for the issuance of certificates.



How do I know that my certificate is valid if it does not have a signature?
Our certificates don't have a signature, however, you will see a QR code at the bottom left. The QR Code, when scanned, will direct you to a page where you can verify that you have completed the course. This is to prevent other people from reproducing the certificate and editing their name into it.



Do you require a Return of Service after completing a course?
No. There is no Return of Service for completing a UPOU MOOC.
